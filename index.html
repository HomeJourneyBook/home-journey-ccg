Rules

The Idea

Two players. One device. Tea defends the Tavern, Jeet defends the Core — each with 20 HP. You build a field of Travelers and Legendaries, cast spells, activate artifacts and worlds. Bring your opponent's base to zero first.

What's On a Card

Every card shows: its cost (top left corner — how much Essence to play it), its art in the center, its name, and for creatures — HP (heart) and ATK (sword) at the bottom. Below that is its ability text describing what it does.

Example: a Szarg costs 1 Essence, has 1 HP and 2 ATK, and the text reads "Vanguard" — meaning it can act the turn it arrives.

Essence

Essence is your resource. You start with a maximum of 1 and gain +1 each turn — so by turn 5 you have 5 Essence to spend. It fully refills at the start of every turn. You can never gain more than your current maximum in a single turn (unless a card says otherwise).

Once per turn you may burn a card from your hand: it goes to the Void forever, but your Essence maximum permanently increases by 1. Useful when you have cards you don't need and want to reach expensive cards faster.

A Turn, Step by Step

1. Your Essence refills. Worlds, Artifacts, and field effects trigger automatically (draws, heals, regen, etc.).

2. You act freely — play cards, attack with creatures, use active abilities — in any order, as many times as you have Essence and available creatures.

3. Press End Turn (or hit Space) when done. You draw 1 card (plus any extras from your effects), then your opponent takes over.

Creatures

Travelers and Legendaries are creatures. When played, they enter the battlefield in a sleeping state (dashed border) and cannot act until your next turn. To attack with a creature: tap it to select it, then tap the enemy you want to hit.

When a creature attacks, the defender hits back simultaneously — unless it's Feared or Invisible. Creatures killed in battle go to the Graveyard (☠ counter) and may be revived. Burned cards go to the Void and are lost forever.

Spells

Spells take effect the moment they're played — draw cards, revive a fallen ally, add bonus Essence — then disappear into the Void. They leave no card on the battlefield.

Worlds

A World is a permanent card that sits below your battlefield and passively affects the game as long as it's active. You can only have one World at a time. Playing a second World replaces the first — the old one goes to the Void. Worlds activate at the start of each turn.

Artifacts

Artifacts also sit below the battlefield, but up to two can be active at once. Some trigger automatically each turn (draw, heal). Others are active — tap them to use their ability once per turn. Artifacts sleep their first turn, just like creatures.

Graveyard vs Void

The Graveyard (☠) holds only creatures killed in battle. They can be revived by certain cards. The Void holds burned cards, played spells, and replaced worlds — nothing can retrieve them.

Glossary of Terms

Terms appear on cards in plain language.

Vanguard

This creature can act — attack or use abilities — on the same turn it enters the field.

Sleeping

A creature that entered the field this turn and cannot act yet. Shown with a dashed border. It wakes up at the start of your next turn. Vanguard creatures skip the sleeping state entirely.

Provoke

All enemy attacks must target this creature. A Feared Provoke creature still blocks — it cannot attack but enemies must still target it.

Pierce

This creature ignores Provoke and can attack the enemy base or any enemy directly. Pierce does not bypass Bushido.

Fear

When this creature attacks, the target is Feared: it skips its next turn and deals no counter-attack damage. A Feared creature cannot attack but Provoke still applies.

Invisible

This creature cannot be chosen as an attack target as long as other allies are on the field. When it becomes the last creature, it can be targeted normally. It also takes no counter-attack damage when attacked.

Rage

Each time this creature attacks, it permanently gains +1 ATK. The bonus resets if it dies and is revived.

Burn

When this creature attacks, the target catches fire. Burning creatures lose 1 HP at the start of every turn. Fire is permanent until removed or death.

Regen

This creature restores X HP to itself at the start of each turn, up to its maximum HP.

Aura

A passive field effect that benefits all allies while this card is on the field. The bonus disappears immediately when the aura card leaves the field. Multiple aura sources stack.

Max HP

Some abilities increase a creature's maximum HP. If the creature is at full health when this happens, its current HP also rises (3/3 becomes 4/4). If it's already wounded, only the maximum grows (2/3 becomes 2/4).

Squad

When 3 or more Travelers of the same type of Gate are on your field simultaneously, they all receive a squad bonus. The bonus activates automatically and is lost the moment the squad drops below 3.

Revive

Bring a creature back from the Graveyard at full HP. It enters the field in a sleeping state and is ready to act next turn. Revived creatures do not trigger On Enter effects.

Raise

A special form of revival — the creature returns with only 1 HP, all its abilities intact, in sleeping state. Unlike Revive, Raise does not restore full health. Used by Phlegmor each turn automatically.

On Turn Start

This effect triggers automatically at the start of your turn, before you take any actions. Examples: Regen restores HP, Worlds and Artifacts provide their bonuses, Phlegmor raises a creature.

On Enter

This effect triggers the moment the card is played from hand to the field. It does NOT trigger when a creature is revived or raised from the graveyard.

On Attack

This effect triggers each time this creature attacks — whether targeting an enemy creature or the base.

On Any / Own Death

This effect triggers whenever a creature dies on the battlefield — by any cause. On Any Death counts all creatures; On Own Death counts only yours.

On Play Creature

This effect triggers each time you play a creature card from your hand to the field.

Lore

Before This

The Archive exists. Somewhere in the Valley, Teantist still brews Chaetron and translates the Book — page by page, year by year. Shen still tends the Tavern at the crossroads of all Worlds. Travelers still walk the Path, collecting what remains of vanished civilizations.

That is the record. What follows is what the record did not anticipate.

The Fracture

No one knows exactly when Jeet stopped being a slow erosion and became something else. The change was not announced. It was noticed in gaps — a World that Shen stopped receiving reports from. A Traveler who returned speaking in patterns that did not belong to them. A section of the Archive that Teantist quietly sealed.

By the time the Tavern held a council, Jeet had already claimed a third of the known Worlds. Not destroyed them. Claimed them. The Travelers inside did not die. They remained — but they walked for Jeet now.

Two Sides

The line did not form cleanly. Some Travelers chose. Others were taken. A few — Ryvlen, the Reaper — had walked so close to Jeet for so long that no one was entirely surprised when they stopped returning to the Tavern.

What remained of the Path split into two currents. Those who still walked toward the Great Return, toward the Essence, toward Tea. And those who now walked deeper into dissolution — whether by choice, corruption, or something in between that the Archive had no word for.

What Is at Stake

The Worlds between them are the contested ground. Each one holds memories — fragments of what civilizations once knew, preserved in the imprints that Unique Travelers are built to carry. If Jeet claims enough of them, the Great Return becomes impossible. Not because the Essence disappears. Because there will be nothing left that remembers what it was.

Teantist has not sealed the Archive. He continues to send Schemes. Whatever he found in those pages he will not translate — he has apparently decided it does not change what must be done next.

Now

Two forces move through the remaining Worlds. One carries tea, memory, and the long patience of those who still believe the Path leads somewhere worth reaching. The other carries the strange clarity of dissolution — the knowledge that entropy, too, has its own direction.

The outcome of this is not recorded yet.
