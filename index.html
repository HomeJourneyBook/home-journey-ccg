Rules

Objective

Two players face off — Tea defends the Tavern, Jeet defends the Core. Each base starts at 20 HP. Reduce your opponent's base to zero to win. The game is played on a single device, passing it between turns.

Setup & Mulligan

Each player starts with 5 cards drawn from their shuffled deck. Before taking your first turn, you may mulligan — reshuffle your hand into the deck and redraw.

You get up to 3 mulligans per game: the first is free (draw 5 cards), the second costs 1 card (draw 4), the third costs 2 cards (draw 3). The Mulligan button appears only on your first turn.

Turn Structure

Each turn follows this order: your Essence maximum increases by 1 and fully refills. Passive abilities trigger (Worlds, Artifacts, field cards). You draw 1 card. Then you may play cards, attack, and use abilities in any order. Press End Turn when done.

Essence

Every card has an Essence cost shown in the top corner. You start with 1 Essence on turn 1 and gain +1 maximum each turn — so on turn 3 you have 3/3. Essence fully refills every turn. You can never have more Essence than your current maximum.

Once per turn, you may Burn a card from your hand to permanently increase your Essence maximum by 1. Burned cards go to the Void and are gone forever — they cannot be revived.

Your Hand

Tap a card to preview it. While previewed, you'll see two buttons above it: Play (if you can afford it) and Burn (if you haven't burned yet this turn). Tap the card again to put it back. Tap elsewhere to cancel.

Playing Creatures

Creatures enter the battlefield in a Sleeping state — they cannot act on the turn they are played (shown with a dashed border). On your next turn they wake up and can attack or use abilities. Exception: creatures with Vanguard can act immediately.

When a creature attacks, the defender always strikes back — even if it dies from the attack. Both creatures deal their ATK as damage simultaneously.

Attacking the Base

To attack the enemy base, select a creature on your field then tap the opponent's stats bar (it glows red when available). The base cannot be attacked if the enemy has a creature with Provoke or Bushido on the field.

Graveyard & Void

Creatures killed in battle go to the Graveyard (☠ counter). Some cards can revive creatures from the graveyard. Burned cards and cards discarded by spells go to the Void — they cannot be retrieved by any means.

Worlds & Artifacts

Worlds and Artifacts are permanent cards placed on the battlefield. You can only have one World active at a time — playing a new one replaces the old. Artifacts stack (up to 2). Both trigger their effects at the start of your turn.

Spells

Spells trigger instantly when played and go to the graveyard. They have no body on the battlefield.

Keywords

Vanguard

This creature can attack on the same turn it enters the battlefield.

Provoke

All enemy attacks — including attacks on the base — must target this creature first. A feared Provoke creature still blocks. Multiple Provoke creatures each draw attacks.

Bushido (Nabunagi only)

All attacks — including Pierce — must target this creature. Bushido overrides Provoke and applies even while Feared.

Pierce

This creature ignores Provoke and can attack the base directly. Pierce does not bypass Bushido.

Fear

When this creature attacks, the target skips its next turn. A Feared creature cannot attack but Provoke still applies — it still blocks attacks.

Burn 🔥

When this creature attacks, the target catches fire. Burning creatures lose 1 HP at the start of every turn. The fire is permanent until removed. An Orbiton heal removes Burn.

Heal: X

Active ability. Select this creature, then tap a wounded ally to restore X HP and remove all debuffs (Fear and Burn). Can also attack normally instead.

AOE: X

Active ability. Select this creature, then use the ability button to deal X damage to all enemy creatures at once.

Draw: X

Draw X extra cards — either each turn (passive) or on attack (trigger), depending on the card.

Regen: X

This creature restores X HP to itself at the start of each turn.

HP vs Max HP

Some abilities increase Max HP. If a creature is at full HP when its Max HP increases, its current HP also increases (e.g. 3/3 becomes 4/4). If it is wounded, only the maximum grows (e.g. 2/3 becomes 2/4).

Empty Deck

If your deck is empty, you simply do not draw cards. There is no penalty — the game continues until a base reaches zero.

📖 Lore

Before This

The Archive exists. Somewhere in the Valley, Teantist still brews Chaetron and translates the Book — page by page, year by year. Shen still tends the Tavern at the crossroads of all Worlds. Travelers still walk the Path, collecting what remains of vanished civilizations.

That is the record. What follows is what the record did not anticipate.

The Fracture

No one knows exactly when Jeet stopped being a slow erosion and became something else. The change was not announced. It was noticed in gaps — a World that Shen stopped receiving reports from. A Traveler who returned speaking in patterns that did not belong to them. A section of the Archive that Teantist quietly sealed.

By the time the Tavern held a council, Jeet had already claimed a third of the known Worlds. Not destroyed them. Claimed them. The Travelers inside did not die. They remained — but they walked for Jeet now.

Two Sides

The line did not form cleanly. Some Travelers chose. Others were taken. A few — Ryvlen, the Reaper — had walked so close to Jeet for so long that no one was entirely surprised when they stopped returning to the Tavern.

What remained of the Path split into two currents. Those who still walked toward the Great Return, toward the Essence, toward Tea. And those who now walked deeper into dissolution — whether by choice, corruption, or something in between that the Archive had no word for.

What Is at Stake

The Worlds between them are the contested ground. Each one holds memories — fragments of what civilizations once knew, preserved in the imprints that Unique Travelers are built to carry. If Jeet claims enough of them, the Great Return becomes impossible. Not because the Essence disappears. Because there will be nothing left that remembers what it was.

Teantist has not sealed the Archive. He continues to send Schemes. Whatever he found in those pages he will not translate — he has apparently decided it does not change what must be done next.

Now

Two forces move through the remaining Worlds. One carries tea, memory, and the long patience of those who still believe the Path leads somewhere worth reaching. The other carries the strange clarity of dissolution — the knowledge that entropy, too, has its own direction.

The outcome of this is not recorded yet.
